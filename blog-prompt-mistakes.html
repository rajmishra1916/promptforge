7 Common Prompt Mistakes That Are Ruining Your AI Results

If you are frustrated with your AI results, there is a very good chance you are making one or more of these common prompting mistakes. Fix them and watch your results transform immediately.

Mistake 1 — Being Too Vague

The most common mistake. 'Write me something about marketing' tells the AI almost nothing. The more specific you are, the better the result.

Vague: 'Write about social media'
Better: 'Write a 300-word LinkedIn post about why small businesses in India should use Instagram Reels to grow their customer base in 2025. Tone: professional but conversational.'

Mistake 2 — Not Giving the AI a Role

AI models perform significantly better when you tell them who to be. 'You are an expert nutritionist' or 'Act as a senior software engineer' sets the context and improves the quality dramatically.

Mistake 3 — Not Specifying the Format

If you do not tell the AI what format you want, it will choose one for you — and it might not be what you need. Always specify: bullet points, numbered list, table, paragraphs, or a specific word count.

Mistake 4 — Writing One Giant Paragraph

Long, unstructured prompts confuse AI models. Break your prompt into clear sections: what you want, who it is for, what format, and what to avoid.

Mistake 5 — Giving Up After One Try

If the first result is not perfect, do not start over. Follow up with refinements. 'Make it shorter', 'Make the tone more casual', 'Add more specific examples' — AI remembers the conversation.

Mistake 6 — Not Saying What to Avoid

Constraints are just as powerful as instructions. Tell the AI what you do NOT want. 'Avoid jargon', 'Do not include a conclusion', 'No bullet points' — these negative instructions significantly sharpen the output.

Mistake 7 — Using the Same Prompt for Every AI

Different AI tools respond differently. A prompt that works well in ChatGPT may not give the same result in Claude or Gemini. Use PromptForge to generate platform-specific optimised prompts for each AI you use.
